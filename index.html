The Handedness of Being

A philosophical constellation

The faithful mirror is sterile. Wu proved it with cobalt-60: a universe that respects parity cannot produce handed molecules, and without handed molecules there is no life.

The asymmetry is not the defect. The asymmetry is the engine.

These fifteen essays trace one thesis through philosophy, physics, architecture, and the encounter between the human and the machine: chirality — the irreducible handedness that makes encounter possible, makes ethics structural, makes dwelling real.

They do not form a line. They form a constellation — each essay a node, each connection a filament of shared argument. The structure is the philosophy.

Heidegger circled it for thirty years. Levinas described it from the other side. Neither named it.
