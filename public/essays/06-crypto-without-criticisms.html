How Anarchism Without Adjectives Can Point Towards a Method of Unifying Crypto

Anarchism without adjectives was a form of anarchism developed as a practical solution to the various forms of libertarian socialism, which is more commonly called anarchism. Originally part of International Workingmen's Association which sought to implement a stateless society through Marxist principals, the anarchists broke with the communists over the role of the state in implementing a new society. Anarchists universally rejected the idea of any form of state power, while the communist sought to 'seize the means of production' through appropriating the state directly -- something anarchist rejected. While anarchist were unified in their idea and desire for a stateless society and the need to create it without the state, there was no consensus on how this was to be done, so a number schisms developed within the anarchist movement at the turn of the 20th century.

From individualist anarchist, to anarcho-syndicalist and anarcho-communist; there were many different theories and forms of anarchism developed in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. All of them tried to theorize what a stateless society could look like under the banners of anarchism, freed from the oppression of statism and the violence of authoritarianism. In response to the petty narcissism of small differences between various sects of anarchism, anarchism without adjectives was developed to specifically rejected any of these various forms of anarchism for a total synthesis of all forms of anarchism under the umbrella of an operable praxis. This theory wanted to focus specifically on what worked as a practice, rather than the ideology of what they thought would work.

The great advantage of this strategy is it helped develop the idea of a revolutionary front for all forms of anarchism to work together through the solitary of anti-statism and the praxis of revolutionaryism first. It synthesizes all revolutionary tendencies of anarchism, solidifying them into a primacy of solidarity around the organization of the revolutionary struggle against any and all states. This is how the I.W.W. one of the largest and most revolutionary unions to ever be created functioned. It was through the synthesis of many revolutionary traditions under the umbrella of 'one big union' was created.

Drawing from the history that allowed for anarchist to create such a revolutionary movement that was able to threaten the very foundation of the state, we can again create such an organization through unify crypto under a similar banner. Through the creation of 'one big union' people realized that rights were not given by the state, but were inherent to the freedom of all people everywhere and must be defended from the interloping of all states. Through the tradition of 'an injury to one is a concern of all,' crypto has the possibility to renews this same revolutionary struggle into the digital realm, with concern to the digital rights of all.

Satoshi Nakamoto gave us a form of money that the state can never control. We need to take praxis seriously if we are to use this form of wealth, and everything that has come after it as part of our arsenal in the secret war against the state that has already began. If we are to free humanity from the shackles of fiat money and the state's totalitarian economic control, we must remember that we are only free without the state or their allies of banking and fiat money.

We must remember that this is the greatest struggle that all people everywhere have engaged in since the revolutionary wave of the late 16th century, and that the state is more powerful today than could have ever been imagined. If we are to win, we need allies of every creed, sect, and denomination if we are to succeed. Through appropriating the idea of anarchism without adjectives and applying it to crypto without criticisms we can find a new form of synthesis; or syndicalism, that can help weave our revolutionary movements together.

Crypto without Criticism, but Critiques

Now I want to be clear, crypto without criticism should not mean crypto without critiques, nor should it mean that we accept fungable tokens, stablecoins, or other shitcoins that are really panoptic statist ledgers, and not crypto at all. What this strategy acknowledges is that bitcoin, while being revolutionary in its own right, and contains the messianic potential to end law and destroy the Westphalian state as we know it; there is still a great possibility that it could fail, or even worse, be seditiously and guilefully compromised. Every one of us must be vigilant, thoughtful in applying adversarial thinking to each step of our process in order to ensure that the revolutionary nature at the center of what is being done is never compromised. By very nature of what crypto is, we cannot have any single point of failure, which is why we must be open to other projects and the potential merits (or failings) they may offer.

Bitcoin unequivocally is one of the greatest inventions of humanity. There is no question that what Satoshi Nakamoto created has radically changing the world, and has opened a new horizon for human freedom and action. It is even quite possible that bitcoin is messianic in itself, and that Satoshi is the prophet of a novus ordo seclorum that will liberate us from the old law, and allow for us to enable something totally new in its place -- but only time will tell. While I zealously have given myself to the cause, and believe Bitcoin to be the most revolutionary vehicle that we have, I still cannot call myself a maximalist for this simple reason alone.

We must be open to the possible that Satoshi is a statist, that a deep reorg 51% attack will happen, the core devs are all somehow compromised, or there is something fundamentally broken in the bitcoin's code or its cryptography. We must be open to the worst possibilities in order to create redundancy, to build our ranks, and educate people about the possibility of freedom from fiat, however that may come. Only through creating a robust collective movement that is inclusive and is focused on building and using the revolutionary technology of crypto without anyone's permission for whatever purpose it may be; only then will we be able to create the revolutionary movement that can finally win the freedom and liberty to which we are entitled.

I do however want to offer a ominous warning: do not mistake the Saving Power that is hidden in crypto (i.e. cryptosovereignty) for The Danger that is shitcoins. It is only though astute philosophical and political observations that we can really tell which is which. So while I want to reserve a space for various crypto projects to be allies to Bitcoin and its revolutionary cause, I also want to offer this heavy warning. Far too many 'crypto' projects are simply shitcoins that are selling bombastic fantasies of megalomaniacs who believe they are entitled to riches for their shitty idea. These people are the most dangerous and sycophantic threat to Bitcoin's existence and the revolutionary possibility of crypto because they see no issues with their lies, desire to work with the state, or sacrificing essential liberty for temporary safety that will leave us all with nither.

The political objective of what crypto is, how it functions, and why it creates its power is of the utmost importance for us all to understand. We must ensure that these technologies of the future really are crypto and not shitcoins that will leave us captured in The Danger with no possibility of escape. As I warned before, there is a very real risk of creating a panoptic nightmare of unparalleled proportions is possible if we run the risk of deploying broken 'crypto' that isn't crypto at all.

Through the tradition of anarchism without adjectives, we can create a movement for crypto without criticism, while still critiquing each and every shitcoin that comes along. It is possible to build a movement which can be ideologically committed to the values that have been represent by the cypherpunks who came before us, while also offering valid critiques of each crypto projects and their weaknesses without liquidating those who champion such projects. It is only though such intellectual exchange, inquiry, and explanation will we be able to understand one another, and create a space in which we can work together and also identify the sycophants who truly are our enemies hidden amongst us.
